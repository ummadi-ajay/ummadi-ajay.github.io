# Outreach Strategy

## Positioning

Pitch MakerWorks Lab as a South Asia robotics proving ground, not as a donation seeker.

The strongest message:

> MakerWorks Lab can put your hardware into the hands of serious young builders in Mumbai, test it under real robotics workloads, document the integration, and create credible education and competition stories around it.

Add an open-source-first angle wherever relevant:

> MakerWorks Lab works mostly with open-source hardware, open robotics tools, and maker-friendly ecosystems. We are keen to try your products in our student projects, collaborate with your team, and recommend strong hardware through our official channels when it performs well in real builds.

## What suppliers care about

Hardware vendors usually care about one or more of these outcomes:

- Their hardware being adopted by students before they become engineers.
- Their hardware being trusted by an open-source-focused robotics lab.
- Real feedback from edge cases they do not see in internal testing.
- Public project evidence: build logs, photos, videos, GitHub repos, tutorials, competition robots.
- Credible recommendations through official lab channels after hands-on testing.
- India market entry, distribution, or education footprint.
- Low-friction logistics and a reliable recipient who will not disappear after receiving hardware.

## Ask ladder

Start small, make the first yes easy, then expand.

| Tier | Ask | Best for | What MakerWorks offers |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | 1-3 engineering samples, beta boards, sensors, firmware access | DevRel, product managers | 30-day technical report, integration notes, bug list, test media |
| 2 | B-stock, older stock, education kits, open-box units | CSR, marketing, regional sales | Student projects, lab displays, competition use, approved logo credit |
| 3 | Educational discount or bulk pricing | Sales, distributors | Repeat purchasing, parent/student adoption, local workshops |
| 4 | Named lab/competition partnership | Marketing, education programs | Co-branded showcase, demo day, case study, social posts |

## What to ask for by category

- Microcontrollers and dev boards: sample boards, beta firmware, classroom bundles, early SDK access.
- Edge AI hardware: Jetson kits, OAK cameras, AI accelerator boards, thermal test support, sample models.
- Sensors: LiDARs, depth cameras, IMUs, GNSS, ToF sensors, encoder kits.
- Motion hardware: smart servos, motor drivers, BLDC controllers, gear motors, mecanum systems.
- Structural systems: FTC/VEX-style build systems, fastener kits, bearings, channels, robot chassis.
- 3D printing: printers, spare parts, filament, maintenance kits, classroom pricing.
- Semiconductor vendors: evaluation kits, sample ICs, training credits, university program access.

## Proof points to lead with

Use these only after confirming the exact details:

- MakerWorks Lab is based in Malad East, Mumbai.
- Students work on robotics, electronics, Arduino, Raspberry Pi, Python, AI, IoT, 3D design, and real-world engineering projects.
- Programs cover ages 8 to 17.
- The lab prepares students for WRO, VEX IQ Robotics, Coolest Projects, and Mekathlon.
- Existing site material mentions more than 30 students and multiple competition tracks.
- If confirmed: students selected for WRO International Championships in Panama and Manila.
- If confirmed: students participated in Coolest Projects organized by the Raspberry Pi Foundation in Hyderabad.

## Offer menu

Give vendors a concrete deliverable menu. This makes the request feel professional.

- 30-day evaluation report with setup steps, test conditions, failure modes, photos, and recommendations.
- Integration repo with sample code and wiring notes when public sharing is allowed.
- One technical feedback call with their engineering or DevRel team.
- Student build showcase with approved brand mention.
- Product recommendation through MakerWorks official channels after successful testing.
- Lab demo day or workshop using their hardware.
- Competition robot placement where rules allow.
- India education-market feedback from students, parents, and mentors.

## Logistics wording

Use this to reduce friction:

> We can receive shipments in Mumbai and can also work through your Indian distributor or reseller if that is easier. If needed, we can provide lab details, GST/import information, and a written acknowledgement that the hardware is for educational evaluation and non-resale use.

Do not promise duty exemption unless it is already arranged. Say "we can handle local logistics" rather than "shipping is free/easy."

## Outreach cadence

Day 0: Send the personalized first email or contact form.

Day 2: Connect with the relevant DevRel, education, sales, or partnerships person on LinkedIn.

Day 5 business days: Short follow-up with one concrete pilot proposal.

Day 12 business days: Final follow-up with a lower-friction ask, such as educational discount or 1 sample unit.

Day 30: If no response, re-enter later with a specific student project or competition milestone.

## What not to do

- Do not lead with "donation" as the first word.
- Do not send the same generic email to every company.
- Do not ask for expensive equipment before offering a small pilot.
- Do not promise logos on competition robots unless the competition rules allow it.
- Do not mention unverifiable awards or inflated student numbers.
